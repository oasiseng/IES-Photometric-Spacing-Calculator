Methodology

Average maintained illuminance, minimum, maximum, and uniformity ratios are computed by direct point-by-point integration of the user-supplied manufacturer IES file across a regular grid spanning one full pole-to-pole repeating module of pavement. Per-point illuminance follows the inverse-square cosine law with bilinear interpolation on the candela web:

E(x,y) = Σi [ I(γi, Ci) · cos(γi) / di2 ] · LLF

where I(γ,C) is the candela value interpolated from the IES distribution at vertical angle γ and lateral angle C; d is slant distance from the luminaire to the pavement point; and the summation runs over all luminaires within the influence zone (±2 poles per side). Photometric data is applied at full candela multiplier with TILT=NONE per IES LM-63. The grid is sampled at 40 longitudinal × 11 transverse zone-center points per module. Methodology consistent with the procedures used by DIALux and AGi32 for representative-section roadway analysis.
